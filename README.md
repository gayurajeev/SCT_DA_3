📊 IBM HR Analytics — Employee Attrition Dashboard

🧠 Problem Statement

Employee attrition is a major challenge for organizations, leading to increased recruitment costs, loss of productivity, and reduced operational efficiency.

The objective of this project is to analyze IBM HR employee data to understand:

Why employees are leaving the company
Which departments and roles have the highest attrition
What factors influence employee turnover (overtime, income, satisfaction, etc.)
How HR teams can identify high-risk employees and improve retention strategies

🎯 Project Objective

To build an interactive HR analytics dashboard that helps stakeholders:

Track overall attrition trends
Identify key drivers of employee turnover
Analyze attrition across departments, roles, and demographics
Support data-driven HR decision-making

Age Group
Income Category (if applicable)

3. Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
Attrition distribution analysis

4. Data Visualization (Tableau)

5. Dashboard Publishing
Published dashboard on Tableau Public for interactive access

📈 Key Insights
Employees working overtime show significantly higher attrition rates
Certain job roles (e.g., Sales and Laboratory roles) have higher turnover
Lower income employees are more likely to leave the organization
Employees with low job satisfaction show higher attrition probability
Early-career employees tend to leave the company more frequently

💡 Business Recommendations
Reduce excessive overtime workload for employees
Improve compensation structure for low-income groups
Strengthen retention strategies for high-risk job roles
Conduct regular employee satisfaction surveys
Improve onboarding and engagement for early-career employees
